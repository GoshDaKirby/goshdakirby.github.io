What Is CDI?

Clinical Documentation Improvement

Clinical Documentation Improvement (CDI) is the process of reviewing and improving inpatient medical record documentation to ensure it accurately reflects the patient's true severity of illness and complexity of care.

Our Certified CDI Specialists work concurrently with clinical staff to identify documentation gaps, initiate physician queries, and drive quality data that supports appropriate reimbursement and risk adjustment.

Agency CDI Nurses are also available for PRN (as-needed) engagements — please contact one of our Certified Clinical Document Specialists / RNs for expert advice related to documentation improvement and coding.

📋

Documentation Review

Concurrent and retrospective review of inpatient medical records to identify opportunities for documentation improvement.

💬

Physician Queries

Compliant physician query processes that clarify ambiguous, conflicting, or incomplete documentation.

📊

Coding Support

Expert guidance on ICD-10-CM/PCS coding and documentation requirements that support quality metrics and reimbursement.
